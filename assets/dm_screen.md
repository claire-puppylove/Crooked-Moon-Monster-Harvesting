<div class="wide"><h3> Conditions</h3></div>

#### Blinded
***Can't See.*** You can't see and automatically fail any ability check that requires sight.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage, and your attack rolls have Disadvantage.

#### Charmed

***Can't Harm the Charmer.*** You can't attack the charmer or target the charmer with damaging abilities or magical effects.<br>
***Social Advantage.*** The charmer has Advantage on any ability check to interact with you socially.

#### Deafened
***Can't Hear.*** You can't hear and automatically fail any ability check that requires hearing.

#### Exhaustion
***Exhaustion Levels.*** This condition is cumulative. Each time you receive it, you gain 1 Exhaustion level. You die if your Exhaustion level is 6.<br>
***d20 Tests Affected.*** When you make a d20 Test, the roll is reduced by 2 times your Exhaustion level.<br>
***Speed Reduced.*** Your Speed is reduced by a number of feet equal to 5 times your Exhaustion level.<br>
***Removing Exhaustion Levels.*** Finishing a Long Rest removes 1 of your Exhaustion levels. When your Exhaustion level reaches 0, the condition ends.

#### Frightened
***Ability Checks and Attacks Affected.*** You have Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is within line of sight.<br>
***Can't Approach.*** You can't willingly move closer to the source of fear.

#### Grappled
***Speed 0.*** Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** You have Disadvantage on attack rolls against any target other than the grappler.<br>
***Movable.*** The grappler can drag or carry you when it moves, but every foot of movement costs it 1 extra foot unless you are Tiny or two or more sizes smaller than it.

#### Incapacitated
***Inactive.*** You can't take any action, Bonus Action, or Reaction.<br>
***No Concentration.*** Your Concentration is broken.<br>
***Speechless.*** You can't speak.<br>
***Surprised.*** If you're Incapacitated when you roll Initiative, you have Disadvantage on the roll.

#### Invisible
***Surprise.*** If you're Invisible when you roll Initiative, you have Advantage on the roll.<br>
***Concealed.*** You aren't affected by any effect that requires its target to be seen unless the effect's creator can somehow see you. Any equipment you are wearing or carrying is also concealed.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Disadvantage, and your attack rolls have Advantage. If a creature can somehow see you, you don't gain this benefit against that creature.

<br>

#### Paralyzed
***Incapacitated.*** You have the Incapacitated condition.<br>
***Speed 0.*** Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.<br>
***Saving Throws Affected.*** You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage.<br>
***Automatic Critical Hits.*** Any attack roll that hits you is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of you.

#### Petrified
***Turned to Inanimate Substance.*** You are transformed, along with any nonmagical objects you are wearing and carrying, into a solid inanimate substance (usually stone). Your weight increases by a factor of ten, and you cease aging.<br>
***Incapacitated.*** You have the Incapacitated condition.<br>
***Speed 0.*** Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage.<br>
***Saving Throws Affected.*** You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.<br>
***Resist Damage.*** You have Resistance to all damage.<br>
***Poison Immunity.*** You have Immunity to the Poisoned condition.

#### Poisoned
***Ability Checks and Attacks Affected.*** You have Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

#### Prone
***Restricted Movement.*** Your only movement options are to crawl or to spend an amount of movement equal to half your Speed (round down) to right yourself and thereby end the condition. If your Speed is 0, you can't right yourself.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** You have Disadvantage on attack rolls. An attack roll against you has Advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, that attack roll has Disadvantage.

#### Restrained
***Speed 0.*** Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage, and your attack rolls have Disadvantage.<br>
***Saving Throws Affected.*** You have Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws.

#### Stunned
***Incapacitated.*** You have the Incapacitated condition.<br>
***Saving Throws Affected.*** You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage.)

#### Unconscious
***Inert.*** You have the Incapacitated and Prone conditions, and you drop whatever you're holding. When this condition ends, you remain Prone.<br>
***Speed 0.*** Your Speed is 0 and can't increase.<br>
***Attacks Affected.*** Attack rolls against you have Advantage.<br>
***Saving Throws Affected.*** You automatically fail Strength and Dexterity saving throws.<br>
***Automatic Critical Hits.*** Any attack roll that hits you is a Critical Hit if the attacker is within 5 feet of you.<br>
***Unaware.*** You're unaware of your surroundings.

<div class="wide"><h3>Actions</h3></div>

***Attack.*** Attack with a weapon or an Unarmed Strike.<br>
***Dash.*** For the rest of the turn, give yourself extra movement equal to your Speed.<br>
***Disengage.*** Your movement doesn't provoke Opportunity Attack for the rest of the turn.<br>
***Dodge.*** Until the start of your next turn, attack rolls against you have Disadvantage, and you make Dexterity saving throws with Advantage. You lose this benefit if you have the Incapacitated condition or if your Speed is 0.<br>
***Help.*** Help another creature's ability check or attack roll, or administer first aid.<br>
***Hide.*** Make a Dexterity (Stealth) check.<br>
***Influence.*** Make a Charisma (Deception, Intimidation, Performance, or Persuasion) or Wisdom (Animal Handling) check to alter a creature's attitude.<br>
***Magic.*** Cast a spell, use a magic item, or use a magical feature.<br>
***Ready.*** Prepare to take an action in response to a trigger you define. Uses a reaction to activate. Spells using the "Ready" action must be a casting time of an action, uses expended resource to cast it, and requires Concentration.<br>
***Search.*** Make a Wisdom (Insight, Medicine, Perception, or Survival) check.<br>
***Study.*** Make an Intelligence (Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, or Religion) check.<br>
***Utilize.*** Use a nonmagical object.

### Mastery Properties

***Cleave.*** If you hit a creature with a melee attack roll using this weapon, you can make a melee attack roll with the weapon against a second creature within 5 feet of the first that is also within your reach. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon's damage, but don't add your ability modifier to that damage unless that modifier is negative. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.<br>
***Graze.*** If your attack roll with this weapon misses a creature, you can deal damage to that creature equal to the ability modifier you used to make the attack roll. This damage is the same type dealt by the weapon, and the damage can be increased only by increasing the ability modifier.<br>
***Nick.*** When you make the extra attack of the Light property, you can make it as part of the Attack action instead of as a Bonus Action. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.<br>
***Push.*** If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can push the creature up to 10 feet straight away from yourself if it is Large or smaller.<br>
***Sap.*** The creature that is hit by this weapon has Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn.<br>
***Slow.*** If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to it, you can reduce its Speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn. If the creature is hit more than once by weapons that have this property, the Speed reduction doesn't exceed 10 feet.<br>
***Topple.*** If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll and your Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature has the Prone condition.<br>
***Vex.*** If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to the creature, you have Advantage on your next attack roll against that creature before the end of your next turn.<br>

### Long Jump

Move 10+ feet, and jump a number of feet up to your Strength score. When you make a standing Long Jump, you can leap only half that distance. Each foot you jump costs a foot of movement.

### High Jump

Move 10+ feet and jump a number of feet equal to 3 plus your Strength modifier. When you make a standing High Jump, you can jump only half that distance. Each foot you jump costs a foot of movement. <br>You can extend your arms half of your height. Thus, you can reach a distance equal to the height of the jump plus 1½ times your height.

### Concentration

These factors break your Concentration.

**Another Concentration Effect.** You lose Concentration on an effect the moment you start casting a spell that requires Concentration or activate another effect that requires Concentration.<br>
***Damage.*** If you take damage, you must succeed on a Constitution saving throw to maintain Concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage taken (round down), whichever number is higher, up to a maximum DC of 30.<br>
***Incapacitated or Dead.*** Your Concentration ends if you have the Incapacitated condition or you die.

### Tool Proficiency

If you have proficiency with a tool, add your **Proficiency Bonus** to any ability check you make that uses the tool. 
If you have proficiency in a skill that's used with that check (**e.g. Sleight of Hand with Thieves' Tools**), you have ***Advantage*** on the check, too.

### Death Saving Throws

When you start your turn with 0 HP, roll 1d20, and you succeed on a 10+.<br>
**Third Success.** You become Stable.<br>
**Third Failure.** You die.<br>
**Rolling a 1.** Counts as two failures.<br>
**Rolling a 20.** You regain 1 Hit Point.<br>
If you take damage at 0 HP, it counts as a failed Death Save or two failures if the damage is from a Critical Hit.

### Coverage & AC

|Coverage|Benefit|
|:-------|:------|
|Half Cover|+2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws|
|Three-Quarters Cover|+5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws|
|Total Cover|can't be targeted directly|

If behind more than one degree of cover, a target benefits only from the most protective degree. 

### Equipping & Unequipping During Combat

A character can do the following during their turn:<br>
***Free Object Interaction.*** Each character has one free object interaction per turn. Characters can use this to equip/unequip a weapon or item(e.g. sword, staff, wand, bow, etc.) , which includes *picking up/dropping a weapon.*<br>
***Action.*** As part of their action, a character can equip/unequip a weapon or item. *This includes picking up/dropping a weapon.*

In this way, character can unequip a weapon and equip another weapon in their turn. They can only equip/unequip one item (unless they have the **Dual Wielder** feat).

### Visibility Outdoors
When traveling outdoors, most characters can see about 2 miles in any direction on a clear day, except where obstructions block their view. That range increases to 40 miles if they are atop a mountain or a tall hill or are otherwise able to look down on the area from a height. Lightly Obscured conditions reduce visibility: rain reduces maximum visibility to 1 mile, and fog reduces it to between 100 and 500 feet.<br>
***Visibility Underwater***. Visibility underwater depends on water clarity and the available light. Since it is left to DM discretion, I produced the following homebrew rules.<br>
*Clear Water, Bright Light*: 20 ft<br>
*Clear Water, Dim Light*: 10 ft<br>
*Murky Water, Bright Light*: 5 ft<br>
*Murky Water, Dim Light*: 0 ft

### Moonlight and Advanced Darkness
***Home-brew: Advanced Darkness.*** However that is during the day. Druskenvald is covered in Moonlight and Darkness. Entering the Evening Hour (7 pm - 11 pm), darkness engulfs the land in a peculiar way. Torches work normally before the Evening Hours, but after the Creeping Dark sets in their range is reduced, and then further by Crooked Darkness. For example a torch only lights 10 feet of Bright light in Crooked Darkness. Creatures with Darkvision succumb to this advanced darkness with a reduction on their visibility range, and have the disadvantages of Dim Light when the darkness creeps forward. For example, a creature with Darkvision up to 60 ft will only see up to 30 ft in Crooked Darkness without a light source, and only see up to 45 ft in Creeping Darkness.
